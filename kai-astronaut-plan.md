# 🚀 Kai's Road to the Stars
## A 20-Year Training Plan to Become an Artemis Astronaut
**Start Date:** 2026 | **Target Mission:** Artemis 4 | **Launch Age:** ~27

---

## 🌟 The Big Picture

Kai's goal is to be a mission-ready astronaut by approximately age 27, ideally in time for the Artemis 4 mission window. This plan breaks the journey into five distinct phases, each building on the last. Every step is intentional — great astronauts are built through decades of focused effort, curiosity, and discipline.

---

## Phase 1: The Foundation Years (Ages 7–12)
*"Build the love of learning early."*

### Academic Goals
- Maintain **top grades** across all subjects, with special focus on:
  - **Math** (arithmetic → fractions → pre-algebra)
  - **Science** (life science, earth science, introductory physics concepts)
  - **Reading & Writing** (astronauts must communicate clearly in reports and interviews)
- Begin participating in school **science fairs** and **STEM clubs**

### Exploration & Enrichment
- Visit **space museums, planetariums, and air shows** regularly (Kennedy Space Center, Smithsonian Air & Space, etc.)
- Start reading age-appropriate books about space, aviation, and explorers
- Watch NASA launches live and follow missions (e.g., Artemis updates, ISS missions)
- Begin learning **basic coding** (Scratch, then Python basics) — astronauts use software constantly

### Physical Development
- Establish healthy habits: regular exercise, good sleep, nutritious diet
- Try a variety of sports to build coordination, teamwork, and discipline
- Swimming is highly recommended — it's a core part of astronaut training (neutral buoyancy lab)

### Character Goals
- Practice **problem-solving** and **staying calm under pressure** (puzzles, chess, escape rooms)
- Take on **responsibilities** at home to build reliability and leadership mindset

---

## Phase 2: The Academic Acceleration Years (Ages 13–17)
*"Sharpen the mind. Earn the right to fly."*

### Academic Goals
- Pursue the most rigorous courses available:
  - **Algebra, Geometry, Pre-Calculus, Calculus** (by junior/senior year if possible)
  - **Physics, Chemistry, Biology**
  - **AP or IB coursework** to prepare for top universities
- Target a **GPA of 3.9+ / A average** consistently
- Begin **science olympiad**, robotics teams, or aerospace clubs

### Aviation Pathway — Start Here! ✈️
- **Age 14–15:** Begin ground school education (FAA Private Pilot knowledge base)
- **Age 16:** Start **actual flight lessons** with a certified instructor
  - Student Pilot Certificate is available at age 16 in the U.S.
- **Age 17:** Earn the **FAA Private Pilot License (PPL)**
  - Requires: 40 flight hours minimum (20 solo), written exam, checkride
  - This is a major milestone — Kai will be a licensed pilot as a teenager!

### Extracurriculars That Matter
- **Civil Air Patrol (CAP):** The premier youth aviation/aerospace organization in the U.S., offers flight scholarships and aerospace education
- **STEM competitions:** Science Olympiad, FIRST Robotics, Regeneron Science Talent Search
- **Summer programs:** NASA's High School Internship (OSSI), Space Camp (Huntsville, AL), aviation academies
- Volunteer and community service — NASA values well-rounded candidates

### Physical & Mental Development
- Maintain excellent physical fitness (cardiovascular health, strength, flexibility)
- Consider martial arts, swimming, or cross-country to build mental toughness
- Develop stress management skills — high-stakes environments demand calm clarity

---

## Phase 3: College — Building the Expert Foundation (Ages 18–22)
*"Become world-class in your field."*

### Target Universities
Apply to schools with strong aerospace programs, ROTC options, and NASA partnerships:
- MIT, Caltech, Georgia Tech, Purdue, University of Michigan, Stanford
- Air Force Academy or Naval Academy (if military route appeals — many astronauts come from military aviation)

### Recommended Degree Options

**Option A: Aerospace Engineering**
- Focuses on aircraft/spacecraft design, propulsion, aerodynamics, structures
- Very directly applicable to astronaut selection criteria
- Strong job market with NASA, SpaceX, Boeing, Lockheed Martin

**Option B: Astrophysics / Physics**
- Deep theoretical grounding in how the universe works
- Excellent for science-mission astronauts
- Pairs well with a minor in engineering or computer science

> 💡 *Either path works. The key is graduating with honors from a strong program and pursuing graduate study or exceptional work experience immediately after.*

### College Action Plan
- **Maintain GPA 3.7+** — NASA requires at minimum a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution
- **Apply for NASA internships** (NASA's OSSI program, JPL internships, Goddard, Johnson Space Center)
- Consider **ROTC** (Air Force or Navy) — a military commission + flight hours is one of the most direct paths to astronaut selection
- Continue building **flight hours** — work toward:
  - **Instrument Rating**
  - **Commercial Pilot License (CPL)**
  - Ideally accumulate **1,000+ flight hours** over this period (required for astronaut selection)
- Publish or contribute to **research** — scientific literacy and contribution is weighted heavily
- Consider a **Master's degree program** (can be done in 5 years combined B.S./M.S.)

## Phase 4: The Professional Years — Becoming Mission-Ready (Ages 22–26)
*"Get the hours, get the experience, get noticed."*

### Two Primary Pathways

**Military Aviation Path:**
- Commission as a military officer through ROTC or service academy
- Complete pilot training (undergraduate pilot training — UPT)
- Fly tactical jets or military aircraft to build flight hours and experience
- Apply to test pilot school (USAF Test Pilot School, Naval TPS) — highly competitive but a major differentiator for NASA applications
- Accumulate **1,000+ hours in jet aircraft**

**Civilian Engineering/Science Path:**
- Work at NASA, a national lab, SpaceX, Boeing, or a research university
- Lead or contribute to **space mission projects**
- Complete a **Ph.D.** if taking a science track (opens Mission Specialist category)
- Build a portfolio of meaningful technical accomplishment

### Apply to NASA Astronaut Corps
- NASA opens applications roughly **every 2–4 years**
- Requirements at time of application:
  - U.S. citizenship
  - Bachelor's degree in STEM (Master's or Ph.D. preferred)
  - 2+ years of relevant experience OR 1,000+ hours as pilot-in-command of jet aircraft
  - Pass NASA long-duration spaceflight physical
- **Target application window:** Age 25–26
- Selection process takes 12–18 months

---

## Phase 5: Astronaut Candidate Training (Ages 26–27+)
*"The final frontier of preparation."*

### NASA ASCAN Program (~2 Years)
If selected, Kai will begin the **Astronaut Candidate (ASCAN)** program at Johnson Space Center in Houston, TX:

- **T-38 jet proficiency training** — all astronauts must be jet-qualified
- **International Space Station systems training**
- **Spacewalk (EVA) training** in the Neutral Buoyancy Lab (world's largest indoor pool)
- **Russian language study** (Soyuz backup capability)
- **Robotics training** (Canadarm2 operation)
- **Survival training** (water, winter, wilderness)
- **Team leadership and communication** under pressure

### Mission Assignment
- After ASCAN graduation, astronauts enter the **active astronaut corps** and await mission assignment
- With the Artemis program continuing, lunar surface missions are the near-term focus
- **Target: Artemis 4 or contemporaneous lunar mission** — approximately 2033–2035 timeframe
