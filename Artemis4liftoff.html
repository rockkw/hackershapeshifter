03
Phase Three
College — Building the Expert Foundation
Ages 18–22 · 2037–2041

"Become world-class in your field."

Target Universities: MIT, Caltech, Georgia Tech, Purdue, University of Michigan, Stanford — or the Air Force / Naval Academy for the military aviation path.

🛠️ Option A — Aerospace Engineering

  • Aircraft/spacecraft design & propulsion
  • Aerodynamics and structures
  • Directly applicable to NASA criteria
  • Careers: NASA, SpaceX, Boeing, Lockheed

🌌 Option B — Astrophysics / Physics

  • Deep theoretical foundation
  • Ideal for science-mission astronauts
  • Pair with engineering or CS minor
  • Ph.D. track opens Mission Specialist role
🧪
College Action Plan
  • Maintain 3.7+ GPA
  • NASA OSSI internships, JPL, JSC
  • ROTC (Air Force or Navy) — commission path
  • Contribute to published research
🛩️
Flight Hours — Keep Building
  • Earn Instrument Rating
  • Work toward Commercial Pilot License
  • Accumulate 1,000+ flight hours
  • NASA requires 1,000 hrs for pilot path

04
Phase Four
Professional Years — Becoming Mission-Ready
Ages 22–26 · 2041–2045

"Get the hours, get the experience, get noticed."

🪖 Military Aviation Path

  • Commission as military officer via ROTC
  • Undergraduate pilot training (UPT)
  • Fly tactical jets — build hours fast
  • Apply to Test Pilot School (USAF/Navy TPS)
  • Accumulate 1,000+ hours in jet aircraft

🔬 Civilian Engineering Path

  • Work at NASA, JPL, SpaceX, or university
  • Lead or contribute to space mission projects
  • Complete Ph.D. (science astronaut track)
  • Build portfolio of technical achievement
  • Commercial Pilot License + hours

NASA Application Requirements: U.S. citizenship · STEM bachelor's (Master's/Ph.D. preferred) · 2+ years relevant experience OR 1,000+ hours as pilot-in-command of jet aircraft · Pass NASA long-duration spaceflight physical. Applications open every 2–4 years. Target window: Age 25–26.

05
Phase Five
Astronaut Candidate Training
Ages 26–27+ · 2045–2048

"The final frontier of preparation."

🛸
NASA ASCAN Program (~2 Years)
  • T-38 jet proficiency training
  • ISS systems — full certification
  • EVA spacewalk training — Neutral Buoyancy Lab
  • Canadarm2 robotics operation
🌍
Advanced Training
  • Russian language study (Soyuz backup)
  • Water, winter, wilderness survival
  • Team leadership under extreme pressure
  • Mission communication and reporting
🌕
Mission Assignment
  • Graduate ASCAN → active astronaut corps
  • Artemis program: lunar surface focus
  • Target: Artemis 4 or lunar mission
  • Timeline: ~2033–2035 mission window
